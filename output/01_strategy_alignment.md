# Data Strategy and Governance for the Iris Classification Project

## Introduction
The Iris Classification Project aims to classify the various species of iris flowers based on specific metrics such as sepal length, sepal width, petal length, and petal width. The project aligns with our organizational commitment to ensure data integrity, ethical sourcing of data, and supports our long-term business goals of providing reliable and accurate plant classification services.

## High-Level Data Strategy

### Objectives
1. **Data Integrity**: Ensure that all data used in the Iris classification modeling is accurate, reliable, and collected ethically.
2. **Data Accessibility**: Make data available to authorized personnel to foster collaboration and innovation while protecting sensitive information.
3. **Data Quality**: Implement rigorous standards for data validation and cleansing to maintain high-quality datasets.
4. **Data Compliance**: Adhere to industry regulations and organizational policies regarding data usage and protection.

### Key Components
1. **Data Collection**:
   - Source data ethically and transparently.
   - Ensure that all collected data is relevant to the classification task.
  
2. **Data Management**:
   - Maintain a centralized data repository for the Iris dataset.
   - Implement data lifecycle management practices to ensure data remains updated and relevant.

3. **Data Analysis**:
   - Utilize statistical and machine learning techniques to develop predictive models.
   - Regularly validate and test models to ensure accuracy.

4. **Data Security**:
   - Implement data encryption and access controls to protect sensitive information.
   - Conduct regular audits to ensure adherence to security policies.

## Governance Policies

### Data Ownership
- Clearly define data ownership for each segment of the dataset.
- Assign a data steward responsible for maintaining data quality and compliance.

### Data Usage Policy
- Establish guidelines on who can access and use the data.
- Define acceptable use cases to prevent misuse of data.

### Data Sharing and Collaboration
- Promote transparency in data sharing within teams while adhering to privacy standards.
- Foster collaboration by integrating feedback mechanisms for continuous improvement.

### Compliance and Legal Standards
- Ensure compliance with data protection regulations such as GDPR and CCPA.
- Establish protocols for data anonymization where necessary.

## Success Metrics
1. **Data Quality Score**: Establish a scoring system to assess data accuracy, completeness, and consistency.
2. **Model Performance**: Evaluate classification models based on accuracy, precision, and recall metrics.
3. **Compliance Audits**: Carry out bi-annual compliance audits and maintain a record of any findings and resolutions.
4. **User Feedback**: Gather user feedback on data accessibility and quality to continuously improve processes.
