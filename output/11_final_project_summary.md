### Project Objectives
The primary objective was to create a prediction model capable of classifying the species of Iris flowers based on key morphological features. The model had to not only provide accurate predictions but also adhere to ethical AI practices concerning transparency, bias assessment, and regulatory compliance.

### Methodology
- **Model Development:** The Iris Flower Species Predictor was built using a machine learning approach utilizing the well-regarded Iris dataset.
- **Input Features:** The model leverages four traits (sepal length, sepal width, petal length, and petal width) to yield a classification of the flower into one of three species: Setosa, Versicolor, or Virginica.
- **Review Mechanism:** A comprehensive ethical audit was performed to ensure the model's alignment with ethical AI standards.

### Key Findings
1. **Transparency:** 
   - The model architecture is well-documented.
   - Input-output clarity has been maintained throughout the user interfaces.
   - A suggestion for enhancing interpretability of predictions has been identified through tools like SHAP or LIME.

2. **Bias Assessment:**
   - The dataset accounts for class balance, minimizing initial bias risk.
   - Continuous evaluation for algorithmic bias is advised as new data introduces variables, ensuring the model remains robust and fair in predictions.

3. **Compliance:** 
   - The project adheres to ethical standards with respect to data privacy, avoiding the use of PII.
   - Future considerations regarding GDPR compliance are necessary should the model evolve to collect user data.

### Recommendations
- **Implement Interpretability Tools:** Recommendation to include features that clarify decision-making processes such as SHAP and LIME to gain user confidence.
